# Target Users

## Primary Persona: "Armchair Politicians"

**Who they are**:
- Highly interested in politics and societal issues
- Skilled at facilitating consensus in their own contexts (workplace, school, civic organizations)
- See dysfunction in current political system
- Care deeply about finding better solutions
- Would never join current political system despite caring

**Their pain points**:
1. **Powerlessness** - Can't influence politics despite being informed and engaged
2. **Disillusionment** - See problems but no constructive way to participate
3. **Frustration** - Stuck between "vote every 2-4 years" and "run for office" with no middle ground

**Current solutions they use**:
- Social media arguments (unsatisfying, ineffective)
- Signing petitions (feels performative)
- Attending town halls (often devolve into shouting)
- Volunteering for campaigns (supporting others, not their own ideas)

**Why they'll switch to Middling**:
- **Empowerment** - Can propose solutions and get real peer feedback
- **Meaningful participation** - Deliberate on real issues, not just vote
- **Constructive environment** - Platform optimized for consensus, not conflict
- **Regular engagement** - Invited to participate like jury duty
- **Visible impact** - See proposals move from assembly to political consideration

**Demographics**:
- Likely skew younger (but not exclusively)
- Educated and civically engaged
- Across political spectrum (disillusioned exists everywhere)

## Secondary: Politicians

**Who they are**:
- Elected officials at local, state, or national level
- Candidate politicians preparing to run
- Overwhelmed with constituent requests

**Their pain points**:
1. **Constituent overload** - Flooded with "fix my problem" requests
2. **Wrong role expectations** - Expected to do stakeholder analysis and consensus-building, not just vote
3. **No pre-debate infrastructure** - Gap between agenda-setting and voting

**Why they'll use Middling**:
- **Offload constituent work** - Platform handles deliberation and consensus-building
- **Better proposals** - Receive refined, consensus-backed proposals instead of raw complaints
- **Political advantage** - Early support for popular emerging proposals
- **Constituent engagement** - Branded platform shows they're listening and facilitating solutions

**Revenue model**: Subscription for branded Middling site

## Tertiary: Advocacy Organizations & Political Parties

**Who they are**:
- Issue-focused advocacy groups
- Political parties seeking to understand constituents
- Think tanks and policy organizations

**Their pain points**:
1. **Understanding consensus** - Hard to know where society actually stands on issues
2. **Recruiting engaged citizens** - Need pipeline of civically active people
3. **Testing proposals** - Want feedback before launching campaigns

**Why they'll use Middling**:
- **Consensus data** - See where society converges on issues
- **Proposal testing** - Run ideas through citizen deliberation
- **Recruitment pipeline** - Access to engaged, informed citizens
- **Legitimacy** - Citizen-backed proposals carry more weight

**Revenue model**:
- Pay for data access
- Pay for contact access (recruit citizens)

## User Acquisition Strategy

### Phase 1: Viral Hooks
- **Bullspin Detector** - Upload political proposals, AI highlights partisan language
- **Political Spectrum Quiz** - "Where do I fit?"
- Social sharing, quick dopamine hits
- **Goal**: Build initial user base

### Phase 2: Engagement Ladder
- Notify users of new proposals being uploaded
- Light participation (vote/react to proposals)
- Gradually introduce "voice your reasoning" not just like/dislike
- **Goal**: Increase engagement depth

### Phase 3: Assembly Participation
- Invite engaged users to serve on citizens assemblies
- Full deliberation and consensus-building
- **Goal**: Active civic participants

## User Success Criteria

**A user is successful when they**:
- Feel empowered to influence civic issues
- Experience constructive political debate
- See their participation lead to real proposals
- Feel like a meaningful part of the democratic process
- Prefer deliberation on Middling over arguing on social media

## Critical Mass Target

**Initial traction goal**: 250 users by end of December 2025

After Bullspin Detector launch in November, build user base through viral sharing and engagement hooks.
